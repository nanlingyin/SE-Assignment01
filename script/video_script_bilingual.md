## Part 2 - Overview of 6 Models / 六种模型概述 (~4 minutes)

### 1. Waterfall Model / 瀑布模型

**English:**
The Waterfall Model is a traditional and linear software development model. It divides development into fixed stages such as requirements, design, implementation, testing, and maintenance. Each stage must be completed before moving to the next one.

This model is best used when project requirements are very clear and unlikely to change. A good example is government or banking systems with strict documentation and approval processes.

**中文：**
瀑布模型是一种传统的线性开发模型。它把开发过程划分为固定阶段，如需求分析、设计、实现、测试和维护。必须完成前一个阶段后才能进入下一个阶段。

它适合需求清晰且稳定的项目。典型例子包括有严格文档和审批流程的政府系统或银行系统。

---

### 2. Agile Methodology / 敏捷方法论

**English:**
Agile is an iterative and flexible approach to software development. Instead of completing the whole project at once, Agile breaks work into smaller cycles and improves the product continuously through feedback.

Agile is suitable for projects where requirements may change often. A common real-world example is software companies developing web or mobile applications.

**中文：**
敏捷是一种迭代且灵活的开发方法。它不是一次性完成整个项目，而是把工作拆分为更小的周期，并通过持续反馈不断改进产品。

它适合需求经常变化的项目。常见的实际例子是开发 Web 或移动应用的软件公司。

---

### 3. Scrum Framework / Scrum 框架

**English:**
Scrum is a framework within Agile. It organizes work into short time periods called sprints, usually lasting two to four weeks. It also defines roles such as Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development Team.

Scrum is useful for teams that need regular planning, communication, and feedback. It is widely used by software teams in companies like Microsoft and many startup teams.

**中文：**
Scrum 是敏捷体系下的一种框架。它把工作组织为短周期冲刺（Sprint），通常持续两到四周。它还定义了产品负责人（Product Owner）、Scrum Master 和开发团队等明确角色。

它适合需要定期计划和沟通的团队，在像微软这样的公司和许多初创团队中应用非常广泛。

---

### 4. Kanban / 看板法

**English:**
Kanban is a visual workflow management method. It uses a board with columns such as "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done" to help teams track tasks and control work in progress.

Kanban works well for teams that need continuous delivery and flexible task management. It is often used in support teams, maintenance teams, and product development teams.

**中文：**
看板法是一种可视化工作流管理方法。它使用一个包含"待办"、"进行中"和"已完成"等列的看板来帮助团队跟踪任务和控制在制品数量。

它适合需要持续交付和灵活任务管理的团队。常用于技术支持团队、维护团队和产品开发团队。

---

### 5. DevOps

**English:**
DevOps is both a culture and a set of practices that connect software development and IT operations. Its goal is to improve collaboration, automate deployment, and deliver software faster and more reliably.

DevOps is suitable for projects that require frequent releases and stable system operations. Companies like Amazon, Netflix, and Google use DevOps practices extensively.

**中文：**
DevOps 既是一种文化，也是一套实践方法。它把软件开发和 IT 运维连接起来，以提升协作效率、实现自动化部署，并更快更可靠地交付软件。

它适合需要频繁发布并保持系统稳定运行的项目。像 Amazon、Netflix 和 Google 这样的公司都广泛使用 DevOps 实践。

---

### 6. Spiral Model / 螺旋模型

**English:**
The Spiral Model is a risk-driven development model. It combines elements of design, prototyping, and risk analysis in repeated cycles. Each cycle helps the team identify risks and improve the system step by step.

This model is most suitable for large, complex, and high-risk projects. It is often associated with aerospace, defense, and research-based software systems.

**中文：**
螺旋模型是一种以风险为驱动的开发模型。它将设计、原型开发和风险分析元素结合在重复的循环中。每个循环帮助团队识别风险并逐步改进系统。

它最适合大型、复杂且高风险的项目。通常与航空航天、国防和研究类软件系统相关联。

---

## Part 3 - Agile vs Waterfall / 敏捷 vs 瀑布 (~2 minutes)

**English:**
Next, I will compare Agile and Waterfall, because they are two of the most well-known development approaches.

The main difference is how they handle planning and change.

In the Waterfall Model, the project is planned in detail from the beginning. The team follows a fixed sequence of stages, and changes are usually difficult and expensive once development has started. This makes Waterfall more suitable for projects with stable requirements.

In contrast, Agile accepts that change is normal. The team develops the product in small iterations, collects feedback regularly, and adjusts the plan along the way. This makes Agile more suitable for dynamic projects where customer needs may evolve over time.

Another difference is customer involvement. In Waterfall, customers are usually involved at the beginning and at the end. In Agile, customers or stakeholders are involved much more frequently throughout the process.

Documentation is also different. Waterfall usually requires heavy documentation, while Agile focuses more on working software and team collaboration.

In my opinion, Waterfall is better when the work must follow strict procedures, while Agile is better when flexibility and quick response are important.

**中文：**
接下来，我要比较敏捷和瀑布模型，因为它们是最广为人知的两种开发方法。

主要区别在于它们如何处理计划和变更。

在瀑布模型中，项目从一开始就被详细规划。团队遵循固定的阶段顺序，一旦开发开始，变更通常既困难又昂贵。这使得瀑布模型更适合需求稳定的项目。

相比之下，敏捷接受变化是常态。团队通过小迭代开发产品，定期收集反馈，并沿途调整计划。这使得敏捷更适合客户需求可能随时间变化的动态项目。

另一个区别是客户参与度。在瀑布模型中，客户通常只在开始和结束时参与。在敏捷中，客户或利益相关者在整个过程中参与更频繁。

文档也有所不同。瀑布模型通常需要大量文档，而敏捷更注重可运行的软件和团队协作。

在我看来，当工作必须遵循严格程序时，瀑布模型更好；当灵活性和快速响应很重要时，敏捷更好。

## Part 4 - Comparison Table / 对比表讲解 (~2-3 minutes)

**English:**
Now I will walk through my comparison table and highlight several important differences.

**First difference: Flexibility.**
The most flexible models are Agile, Scrum, and Kanban. These models allow teams to adjust priorities and respond to changes quickly. Waterfall is the least flexible because it depends on completing one phase before the next. The Spiral Model is also flexible, but mainly in terms of managing risks and refining the system in cycles.

**Second difference: Customer involvement.**
Agile and Scrum usually have high customer involvement, because feedback is collected frequently. Kanban can also support regular customer feedback, depending on how the team works. Waterfall generally has lower customer involvement after the requirements phase. DevOps focuses more on collaboration between development and operations, but it also helps improve user experience through faster releases.

**Third difference: Documentation level.**
Waterfall usually has the highest level of documentation. This is useful for formal projects with strict control. Agile, Scrum, and Kanban usually have lighter documentation, because they focus more on communication and working results. DevOps documentation often focuses on automation, pipelines, deployment, and monitoring. The Spiral Model may also require strong documentation because of its focus on analysis and risk control.

**Fourth difference: Best suited project type.**
Waterfall is best for stable and predictable projects. Agile and Scrum are ideal for fast-changing software projects. Kanban is good for continuous workflow and maintenance tasks. DevOps is best for products that need frequent deployment and reliable operations. The Spiral Model is best for large and risky projects.

**中文：**
现在我来讲解对比表，强调几个重要的差异。

**第一个差异：灵活性。**
最灵活的模型是 Agile、Scrum 和 Kanban。它们允许团队快速调整优先级和应对变化。Waterfall 最不灵活，因为它要求完成一个阶段才能进入下一个。螺旋模型也有一定灵活性，但主要体现在通过循环来管理风险和完善系统。

**第二个差异：客户参与度。**
Agile 和 Scrum 通常有较高的客户参与度，因为反馈收集很频繁。Kanban 也可以支持定期客户反馈，取决于团队的工作方式。Waterfall 在需求阶段之后通常客户参与度较低。DevOps 更侧重于开发和运维之间的协作，但通过更快的发布也能改善用户体验。

**第三个差异：文档程度。**
Waterfall 通常文档要求最高，这对有严格管控的正式项目很有用。Agile、Scrum 和 Kanban 通常文档更轻量，更注重沟通和工作成果。DevOps 的文档通常聚焦于自动化、流水线、部署和监控。螺旋模型因为注重分析和风险控制，也可能需要较强的文档。

**第四个差异：最适合的项目类型。**
Waterfall 最适合稳定可预测的项目。Agile 和 Scrum 适合快速变化的软件项目。Kanban 适合持续工作流和维护任务。DevOps 最适合需要频繁部署和可靠运行的产品。螺旋模型最适合大型和高风险项目。

## Part 5 - My Choice / 我的选择 (~1-2 minutes)

**English:**
If I had to choose one model for a student team project, I would choose **Scrum**.

The first reason is that Scrum gives a clear structure. Student teams often need deadlines, short-term goals, and regular meetings to stay organized. Sprints make the work easier to divide into manageable parts.

The second reason is communication. Scrum encourages regular discussion, which helps team members solve problems early and keep everyone aligned.

The third reason is flexibility. In student projects, ideas and requirements often change during development. Scrum allows the team to improve the product step by step without losing control.

Overall, I think Scrum is practical, easy to apply, and very suitable for small collaborative teams.

**中文：**
如果我要为学生团队项目选择一个模型，我会选择 **Scrum**。

第一个原因是 Scrum 提供了清晰的结构。学生团队通常需要截止日期、短期目标和定期会议来保持组织性。Sprint 使工作更容易被拆分为可管理的部分。

第二个原因是沟通。Scrum 鼓励定期讨论，帮助团队成员尽早解决问题并保持一致。

第三个原因是灵活性。在学生项目中，想法和需求经常在开发过程中变化。Scrum 允许团队在不失控的情况下逐步改进产品。

总的来说，我认为 Scrum 实用、易于应用，非常适合小型协作团队。
