# Software Development Models - Detailed Comparison Table

## Main Comparison Table

| Criteria | Waterfall | Agile | Scrum | Kanban | DevOps | Spiral |
|----------|-----------|-------|-------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Type** | Model | Methodology | Framework (Agile) | Method (Agile/Lean) | Culture & Practices | Model |
| **Approach** | Linear, sequential | Iterative, incremental | Sprint-based iterations | Continuous flow | CI/CD, automation | Risk-driven, cyclic |
| **Flexibility** | Low | High | High | High | Medium-High | Medium |
| **Customer Involvement** | Low (beginning & end) | High (continuous) | High (every sprint review) | Medium | Medium | Medium-High (each cycle) |
| **Documentation Level** | Heavy | Lightweight | Lightweight | Minimal | Automation-focused | Heavy |
| **Risk Management** | Low (risks found late) | Medium (iterative feedback) | Medium (sprint reviews) | Low | Medium (monitoring) | High (core focus) |
| **Team Size** | Any size | Small to Medium | Small (5-9 recommended) | Any size | Cross-functional | Large teams |
| **Delivery Style** | Single final delivery | Incremental releases | Sprint deliverables | Continuous delivery | Continuous deployment | Iterative prototypes |
| **Feedback Speed** | Slow (end of project) | Fast (each iteration) | Fast (each sprint) | Real-time | Real-time (monitoring) | Per cycle |
| **Change Handling** | Difficult & costly | Welcome & expected | Managed per sprint | Easy (reprioritize) | Automated pipelines | Managed via risk analysis |
| **Best Suited For** | Stable, well-defined projects | Dynamic, evolving projects | Collaborative team projects | Maintenance & support | Frequent release products | Large, high-risk projects |
| **Real-World Example** | Government systems, banking | Web/mobile app startups | Microsoft, Spotify | IT support teams | Amazon, Netflix, Google | Aerospace, defense systems |
| **Key Strength** | Clear structure & documentation | Adaptability & speed | Team communication & rhythm | Visual transparency | Fast delivery & reliability | Systematic risk control |
| **Key Weakness** | Inflexible to changes | Requires experienced teams | Overhead of ceremonies | No fixed timeline | Complex toolchain setup | Expensive & complex process |

## Summary of Key Differences

### Flexibility Ranking (High to Low)
1. Kanban (most flexible - no fixed iterations)
2. Agile (highly flexible - iterative)
3. Scrum (flexible within sprint boundaries)
4. DevOps (flexible deployment, structured pipeline)
5. Spiral (flexible through cycles, but heavy process)
6. Waterfall (least flexible - fixed stages)

### Customer Involvement Ranking (High to Low)
1. Agile (continuous customer collaboration)
2. Scrum (customer feedback every sprint)
3. Spiral (customer review each cycle)
4. Kanban (moderate - depends on setup)
5. DevOps (indirect - through fast releases)
6. Waterfall (minimal - beginning and end only)

### Documentation Requirement Ranking (Heavy to Light)
1. Waterfall (comprehensive documentation required)
2. Spiral (heavy documentation for risk analysis)
3. DevOps (automation scripts and pipeline configs)
4. Scrum (user stories, sprint backlogs)
5. Agile (working software over documentation)
6. Kanban (minimal - board is the main artifact)

### Best Project Type Match
| Project Type | Recommended Model |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Government / Banking system | Waterfall |
| Mobile app startup | Agile |
| University team project | Scrum |
| IT help desk / support | Kanban |
| SaaS product with frequent updates | DevOps |
| Military / aerospace system | Spiral |
